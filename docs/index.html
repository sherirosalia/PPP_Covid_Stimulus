Cash for covid

Where did the Paycheck Protection Program money go?

With the release of small business lending information from the US Treasury in conjunction with the Small Business Association, there is unprecedented access to data that allows a glimpse into the types of buisnesses and people affected by the coronavirus crisis.

Application of big data and machine learning techniques form the basis of the first part of a journey to see what stories are revealed and where to focus granular analysis in order to understand more about this ongoing economic crisis at a human and macro level.

GitHub repository: Cash for Covid, Clean Machine.

Data Source: Small Business Association.

It seems that no section of the country was untouched by small businesses seeking relief from the effects of the shutdown. The visualization below displays a representation male vs. female recipients of loans granted as part of the C.A.R.E.S. act, represents a mere 5% of total since most borrowers declined to state their gender. The size of the bubbles increase with count and males show in blue and women in pink.
